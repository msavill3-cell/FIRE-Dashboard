Key Concepts — Plain English
Backdoor Roth IRA
A legal workaround for high earners who exceed the Roth IRA income limit. You contribute to a Traditional IRA (no deduction at your income), then immediately convert it to Roth. Result: tax-free growth forever, just like a regular Roth. Do this every January.
Advanced Roth Strategy
If Employer A's 401K plan allows after-tax contributions (beyond the $X,XXXK limit), you can contribute up to $X,XXXK more after-tax and then convert it to Roth inside the plan. This is the most powerful tax shelter available to high earners. Check Employer A Your 401K Portal → Plan Details.
HSA — The "Stealth IRA"
Health Savings Account = triple tax advantage: contributions are tax-deductible, grow tax-free, and withdrawals for medical expenses are tax-free. After 65, withdraw for anything penalty-free (like a Traditional IRA). If eligible, it beats both Roth and Traditional IRA. Requires an HDHP health plan. 2025 family limit: $X,XXX.
Tax Loss Harvesting
Selling investments at a loss to offset capital gains taxes. You can also deduct up to $X,XXX/yr against ordinary income. After 31 days, buy back a similar (not identical) fund to maintain your market exposure. You have ~$X,XXX in harvestable losses in Brokerage App right now.
Pro-Rata Rule
The IRS treats all your Traditional IRA money as one pool when converting to Roth. If you have pre-tax Traditional IRA funds, part of your Backdoor Roth conversion becomes taxable. Solution: roll any pre-tax Traditional IRA into your 401K first, then do the backdoor. Confirm Robo-Advisor account is Roth, not Traditional.
X% Rule
A guideline saying you can safely withdraw X% of your portfolio annually in retirement without running out of money over 30 years. Your $X,XXXM target implies $X,XXXK/year. At your $X,XXXK retirement spend target, you need $X,XXXM. Some FIRE planners use X% for longer retirements (50+ years).
